Beaches, Rainforests & a Volcano—All on One Island

Taniti is a small Pacific island (under 500 sq. miles) with varied terrain: sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush rainforest, and a mountainous interior with an active volcano. Around 20,000 residents call Taniti home.

Things to Do

Beaches, rainforest hikes, volcano tours, museums, zip-lining, fishing charters, galleries, arcade, bowling, and more—many centered around Merriton Landing on Yellow Leaf Bay.

Lodging & Booking

From an inexpensive hostel to a 4-star resort, plus family-run hotels and B&Bs. All lodging is regulated and inspected by the Tanitian government.

Getting Around

Public buses serve Taniti City 5 a.m.–11 p.m. daily; taxis and a local car-rental agency are available. Bikes & helmets can be rented; the city is flat and very walkable.

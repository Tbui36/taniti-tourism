Frequently Requested Information

  • Power: 120 volts (same as U.S.).
  • Alcohol: No sales/service from midnight to 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking age: 18 (enforcement is not strict).
  • Language: Many younger Tanitians speak English; little English in rural areas, especially among older residents.
  • Healthcare: One hospital plus several clinics; many multilingual employees.
  • Safety: Violent crime is rare; pickpocketing/petty crime is rising as tourism increases — keep valuables secure.
  • Holidays: Numerous national holidays; some attractions and restaurants close — plan accordingly.
  • Currency: U.S. dollar; many businesses also accept euros and yen. Banks offer currency exchange; major credit cards accepted.

Families

Check holiday closures, carry water on hikes, and use crosswalks in busy areas.

Solo Travelers

Use licensed taxis, share itineraries, and be aware of belongings in crowded spots.

Elder Travelers

Ask about accessibility and shuttle options; carry needed medications and translations if applicable.
